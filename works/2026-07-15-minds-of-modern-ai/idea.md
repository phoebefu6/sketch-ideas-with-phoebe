# The minds of modern AI

## Key Concept

Modern AI was not a single breakthrough. It emerged when decades-old learning ideas finally met large-scale data, parallel computing, and engineering infrastructure. The six pioneers agree that AI already creates real value, but disagree about whether current systems can grow into human-level intelligence.

## Explanation

The visual turns the panel into a four-chapter journey. A neural tree introduces the ideas, data, and compute behind modern AI. An illuminated factory inside a bubble separates real demand from speculative belief in the current paradigm. A bird and airplane show that machine and human intelligence may reach similar capabilities by different means. The final library-city reframes the debate as a practical agenda: build useful systems, explore beyond language, and govern before capabilities accelerate.
